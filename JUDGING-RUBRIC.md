# Judging Rubric, How Your Project Is Scored

Every team presents a short demo to the judging panel, senior executives from leading publishing companies. Each criterion is scored 1 to 5 (1 = Poor, 3 = Meets Expectations, 5 = Exceptional), for a total of 30 points.

## The six criteria

| Criterion | What the judges ask | Score |
|-----------|--------------------|-------|
| **Innovation & Creativity** | How novel and original is the solution? Does it push boundaries beyond existing approaches in publishing? | /5 |
| **AWS Technology Usage** | Effective and meaningful use of AWS services (Amazon Bedrock, Claude Code, and other AWS services or partner integrations). | /5 |
| **Publishing Industry Relevance** | Does the solution address a real, meaningful challenge in the publishing industry (for example personalization, audience engagement, content strategy)? | /5 |
| **Technical Execution** | Is the solution functional, well architected, and technically sound? | /5 |
| **Business Impact & Feasibility** | Is there a clear value proposition? Could this realistically be implemented and scaled? | /5 |
| **Presentation & Communication** | Is the solution clearly explained to both technical and non-technical audiences? | /5 |

**Total: /30**

## Scoring guide

- **25 to 30:** Outstanding, award worthy solution
- **18 to 24:** Strong, solid execution with clear value
- **10 to 17:** Developing, good ideas that need refinement
- **Below 10:** Needs significant improvement

## How to score well, practical tips

- **Innovation:** a fresh twist on a known problem beats a copy of an existing tool. The bonus points in each [idea](ideas/) are innovation fuel.
- **AWS usage:** meaningful beats many. One well used Bedrock call that clearly powers your core feature scores better than five services bolted on for show. Be ready to say *why* you chose each service.
- **Industry relevance:** open your demo with the problem, not the tech. One sentence on who feels this pain and what it costs them, the judges are publishing executives, they know the pain when they hear it.
- **Technical execution:** working beats ambitious. Follow the [Build Guide](BUILD-GUIDE.md), get the minimum demo solid locally first, and cut scope early.
- **Business impact:** end your demo with the value: time saved, revenue protected, readers retained. If you can put a rough number on it, do.
- **Presentation:** you have about 4 minutes. One driver, one narrator works well. Prepare `demo-script.md` (Build Guide Step 6) and have a backup, screenshots of a successful run, in case the live demo misbehaves.
